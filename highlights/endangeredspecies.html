Blue-and-yellow macaw (Ara ararauna)

Ara ararauna (00'04") Mikhail Nilov (2021)

Large neotropical parrot, with one of the highest population of parrots living in captivity. They are popular in aviculture because of their striking color, ability to talk and close bonding to humans.
Blue-and-yellow macaw can live from 30 to 35 years in the wild. They generally mates for life and feed on nuts, seeds, fruits, vegetable matter, bark and leaves and even insects and snails (rarely).


Habitat: South and Central America, island of Trinidad (forest, woodland and savannah in tropical areas).
Size: Height 81-91cm, weight 1.0-1.5kg

Source: Wikipedia (2025)

8 Amazon rainforest endangered species

The Amazon rainforest is the largest in the world and has the richest biodiversity of any terrestrial ecosystem, hosting 10% of all know species. Unfortunately more than 2,300 animals there are at high risk of extinction, due to deforestation, climate change and illegal wildlife trade.
Here are 7 Amazon rainforest endangered species:

  • Jaguar (Panthera onca). Estimated population of less than 15,000 in the wild.
  • Amazonian Manatee (Trichechus inunguis). Classified as vulnerable. There are no accurate population counts.
  • Poison Dart Frog (Dendrobatidae family). Population and status varies significantly across species, many of them are facing critical endangerment.
  • Harpy Eagle (Harpia harpyja). Estimated population of less than 50,000.
  • Golden Lion Tamarin (Leontopithecus rosalia). Only about 3,700 remain in the wild.
  • Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas). Global population under threat, with less than 85,000 nesting females.
  • Giant Otter (Pteronura brasiliensis). Endangered status with an estimated population between 1,000 and 5,000.

Source Stand for trees (2024)
